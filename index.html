Ayass BioScience, LLC is an American biotechnology company headquartered in Frisco, Texas, that develops artificial intelligence software for drug discovery and biomarker identification, and operates a CLIA-certified clinical laboratory.[1][2] The company's research activities have spanned COVID-19 diagnostics,[5] aptamer therapeutics,[7] autoimmune disease profiling,[8] and transcriptome-based approaches to cancer treatment.[9]

Its principal software product, BiRAGAS (Biological Retrieval-Augmented Generation Assessment System), is a platform that applies causal inference methods to multi-omics data with the aim of identifying molecular targets for therapeutic development.[2]

History [edit]

Ayass BioScience was founded by Mohamad Ammar Ayass, a physician with board certifications in pulmonary medicine and nephrology.[1] The company's copyright notices date to 2016.[1] It is based in Frisco, Texas, where it operates both a technology development division and a clinical laboratory.[3]

The company's earliest indexed publications date to 2015, with research on IgA deficiency prevalence and hyperhomocysteinemia as a biomarker.[14][15] During the COVID-19 pandemic, the company published a series of studies on nasopharyngeal biomarkers and aptamer-based therapeutic candidates.[5][6][7]

Technology [edit]

BiRAGAS platform [edit]

BiRAGAS (Biological Retrieval-Augmented Generation Assessment System) is a software platform designed to analyze multi-omics biological data — including transcriptomic, genomic, and perturbation datasets — using computational causal inference methods.[2]

According to the company, the platform constructs directed acyclic graphs (DAGs) representing proposed causal relationships between molecular entities. It integrates four data types: patient expression profiles, GWAS signals and Mendelian randomization estimates, CRISPR perturbation screen data, and curated biological databases including Reactome, KEGG, and the Gene Ontology.[2]

The platform evaluates proposed causal relationships across four validation streams: structure learning algorithms (PC, NOTEARS, FCI), temporal validation via Granger causality, genetic instrument analysis using eQTLs, and functional validation through CRISPR screen data.[2] Each identified relationship is assigned a tiered confidence score.[2]

BiRAGAS offers seven analytical workflows, including causal discovery, directed hypothesis testing, intervention ranking, comparative causality across patient subgroups, counterfactual simulation, and evidence inspection.[4]

Aptamer research [edit]

Ayass BioScience has conducted research on aptamers — short nucleic acid sequences that bind to specific molecular targets. The company has published peer-reviewed studies on aptamers targeting SARS-CoV-2 spike protein variants,[7] thrombin for coagulation applications,[10] immune checkpoint molecules CTLA-4 and NKG2A for cancer immunotherapy,[11] and peanut allergen neutralization.[12][13]

CLIA laboratory [edit]

The company operates a CLIA-certified clinical laboratory that provides transcriptomic profiling and diagnostic testing services, with patient and provider portal access.[3]

Research [edit]

COVID-19 [edit]

During the COVID-19 pandemic, Ayass BioScience researchers published studies on non-invasive nasopharyngeal biomarkers for predicting COVID-19 disease severity. A 2022 study in PLOS ONE identified biomarkers in nasopharyngeal samples as potential predictors of disease outcome.[5] Related work on nasopharyngeal inflammatory lymphocyte perturbation was presented at The Journal of Immunology.[16]

The company also developed DNA aptamers targeting the SARS-CoV-2 spike protein. A 2022 paper in Virology Journal described neutralizing aptamers characterized as having efficacy and safety profiles in in vitro assays.[6] Further aptamer characterization against SARS-CoV-2 variants was published in 2023.[7]

Other published research [edit]

The company's published research includes work on T helper cell profiles and cytokine characterization in autoimmune diseases, published in Methods (Elsevier) in 2023,[8] and a 2024 case report in Cureus describing transcriptome-based treatment selection for melanoma with brain metastasis.[9] Earlier publications include studies on antiphospholipid antibodies as risk factors for COPD,[17] sleep disorders prevalence,[18] and anti-CCP antibody associations.[19]
