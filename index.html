Ayass BioScience, LLC is an American biotechnology and molecular diagnostics company headquartered in Frisco, Texas.[1] The company operates a CLIA-certified clinical laboratory and has published peer-reviewed research in areas including COVID-19 diagnostics,[2][3] aptamer development,[4][5] autoimmune disease characterization,[6] and transcriptome-based cancer treatment.[7]

History [edit]

Ayass BioScience was founded by Mohamad Ammar Ayass, a physician with board certifications in pulmonary medicine and nephrology.[1] The company's earliest indexed publications in peer-reviewed journals date to 2015, with research on immunoglobulin A deficiency prevalence and homocysteine as a pulmonary biomarker.[14][15] The company is based in Frisco, Texas, where it maintains a clinical laboratory and research operations.[1]

Research and publications [edit]

Ayass BioScience researchers have published studies in peer-reviewed journals spanning multiple biomedical fields. The company's publication record, indexed on Google Scholar and PubMed, includes work in virology, immunology, oncology, and respiratory medicine.[2][6][14]

COVID-19 [edit]

During the COVID-19 pandemic, Ayass BioScience researchers published studies on nasopharyngeal biomarkers for predicting disease severity. A 2022 study in PLOS ONE reported the identification of biomarkers in non-invasive nasopharyngeal samples from SARS-CoV-2 patients as potential predictors of disease outcome.[2] Related work on nasopharyngeal inflammatory lymphocyte perturbation in COVID-19 severity prediction was published in The Journal of Immunology.[16]

The group also developed DNA aptamers targeting the SARS-CoV-2 spike protein. A 2022 paper in Virology Journal described neutralizing DNA aptamers with in vitro efficacy and safety profiles.[3] A follow-up study in 2023 characterized high-affinity aptamers against spike protein variants.[4]

Aptamers [edit]

Beyond COVID-19 applications, the company has published aptamer research in several therapeutic areas. A 2023 study in Cells (MDPI) described new high-affinity thrombin aptamers for coagulation applications.[5] In 2024, a study in Cancers (MDPI) reported dual immune checkpoint aptamers targeting CTLA-4 and NKG2A.[8]

The company has also published research on aptamers for peanut allergen neutralization, including a 2024 study in Allergies (MDPI) on modulating immune responses[9] and a 2025 study on aptamer-enhanced surface decontamination for preventing allergen-induced cell degranulation.[10]

Autoimmune and inflammatory diseases [edit]

A 2023 study in Methods (Elsevier) described T helper cell profiles and cytokine characterization approaches for autoimmune disease monitoring.[6] Earlier publications included a 2020 study in Rheumatology on anti-cyclic citrullinated peptide antibodies and their association with rheumatoid factors.[17]

Other published research [edit]

A 2024 case report in Cureus described transcriptome-based treatment selection for a patient with melanoma with brain metastasis.[7] Other publications include studies on antiphospholipid antibodies as risk factors for COPD (CHEST, 2019),[18] sleep disorder prevalence in the US population (SLEEP, 2018),[19] and IgA deficiency correlates (JACI, 2015).[14]

Laboratory operations [edit]

Ayass BioScience operates a CLIA-certified clinical laboratory in Frisco, Texas, offering transcriptomic profiling and diagnostic testing services.[1]
